# JVT Umbrella Growth Map

## Positioning

JVT Technologies is the umbrella company.

It should not present as "we do every kind of AI."

It should present as:

`JVT Technologies builds private AI systems and workflow automation for document-heavy professional teams.`

That positioning is narrow enough to sell, broad enough to expand, and consistent with the current demo and outreach motion.

## Core Public Story

JVT helps teams that deal with sensitive documents, repetitive admin work, and fragmented internal knowledge.

The first promise is simple:

- safer AI use
- faster internal work
- grounded answers
- reviewed automation

## The Rule For Growth

JVT should keep one clear go-to-market wedge while expanding capabilities behind the scenes.

That means:

- narrow public offers
- broad internal capability
- more verticals only when the first wedge is working

## First Three Public Offers

### 1. Private Document Assistant

Best first wedge.

Use for:

- internal document search
- grounded answers with citations
- policy and SOP retrieval
- matter or case support

Best buyers:

- law firms
- estate and probate practices
- tax and accounting firms
- other document-heavy advisory teams

### 2. Inbox And Intake Workflow

Use for:

- shared inbox review
- message triage
- intake summary generation
- draft response preparation with human review

Best buyers:

- firms with overloaded email intake
- teams that lose time on repeated routing and response work

### 3. Workflow Automation Sprint

Use for:

- one concrete operational bottleneck
- one team
- one workflow
- one reviewed automation outcome

Examples:

- packet preparation
- checklist generation
- internal routing
- recurring document assembly

## Secondary Offers

These should exist, but not lead the public story yet:

- internal knowledge assistant
- secure AI deployment advisory
- custom internal operator tools

## Vertical Order

### Now

Push first:

- estate planning
- elder law
- probate
- trust and business-law firms

Why:

- high document density
- high repeatability
- strong privacy need
- strong demo fit
- already aligned with current lead research and product demo

### Next

Expand into:

- accounting firms
- tax firms
- bookkeeping and advisory practices

Why:

- strong process orientation
- recurring document and workflow work
- growing AI interest
- good match for private knowledge and workflow tooling

### Later

Expand carefully into:

- healthcare administrative operations
- medical records and scheduling support
- prior-auth or packet-prep workflows

Why later:

- stronger compliance sensitivity
- more operational complexity
- greater downside if the workflow is weak

## What JVT Should Avoid

- avoid "we can do anything with AI"
- avoid "replace your staff with agents"
- avoid generic AI strategy language
- avoid leading with technology names
- avoid pretending to be a broad SaaS platform already

## Internal Truth vs External Pitch

Internally, JVT may use many agents.

Externally, JVT should sell outcomes.

Examples:

- not "multi-agent orchestration"
- say "reviewed intake and document workflow"

- not "autonomous inbox agent"
- say "shared inbox review and drafting workflow"

- not "custom LLM pipeline"
- say "private internal knowledge assistant"

## Offer Evaluation Rubric

JVT should only add a new offer or vertical if it scores well on most of these:

- high document density
- repeatable workflow pain
- clear privacy or governance need
- easy human-review checkpoint
- short path to a pilot outcome
- credible ROI in weeks, not years

If a new lane fails most of those tests, it should not become the next push.

## Near-Term Recommendation

For the next real selling cycle:

- keep legal as the first outward push
- start building an accounting target list in parallel
- keep healthcare as a later operational expansion
- keep the umbrella-company story, but keep the public entry point tight
