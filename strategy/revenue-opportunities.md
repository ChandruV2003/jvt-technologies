# JVT Technologies Revenue Opportunities

Updated: 2026-05-13

This file is the recurring research ledger for opportunities outside the first private AI document-workflow offer. The rule is simple: pursue service lines that can be delivered by a small AI-assisted team, can reuse the existing M4 control-panel/agent infrastructure, and solve a concrete operational pain.

## Current Signals

- Businesses are using AI but still need help turning it into reliable operations. Zapier-linked reporting in early 2026 emphasized that teams spend real time cleaning up poor AI outputs, which supports a managed-AI-services angle instead of selling generic prompts.
- Voice and speech-native agents are now technically viable for production, but live calling adds provider setup, API cost, consent/compliance, and quality-control requirements.
- Twilio/OpenAI-style voice stacks can support inbound receptionist/intake workflows, but cost and reliability need to be modeled before offering fixed-price unlimited service.
- Reddit/small-business discussions point to setup friction as the real pain: businesses know automation is possible, but they do not want to wire together Gmail, Drive, CRM, forms, invoicing, and AI themselves.

## Immediate Recommendation

Do not chase crypto/trading/NFT-style passive income as a primary business line right now. The highest-confidence path is service revenue from workflow automation because JVT already has working assets: website, outreach engine, control panel, mailbox listener, document demo, and dry-run voice intake.

The next best add-on to the current offer is `Speech-to-text / meeting-to-action packets` because it is easy to demo, has low delivery complexity, and naturally feeds into document workflows.

## 2026-05-13 Reality Check

- Current outbound signal is weak: 108 sent packets, 0 tracked prospect replies, and 51 approved packets queued.
- This does not prove JVT is a bad idea, but it does prove generic private-document-assistant cold outbound should not be the main validation motion.
- The next target should be 5 real owner/operator conversations per week, not more automated sends.
- Prioritize sellable wedges with obvious pain: missed-call intake, meeting-to-action packets, inbox/document triage, and workflow automation cleanup.
- Keep `Private or local AI assistant` as a premium offer, but do not lead with it unless the prospect already has privacy-sensitive document pain.
- Trading/Alpaca experiment should remain paper-only until it has 60-90 days of logged paper results, drawdown tracking, and strict order controls.
- Creating a coin/NFT is not recommended as a revenue line. It requires community, liquidity, trust, distribution, and legal/compliance review; without those, it is closer to speculation than a service business.
