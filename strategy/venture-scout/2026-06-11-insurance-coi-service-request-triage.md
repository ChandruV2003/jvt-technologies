# Venture Scout: Insurance COI And Service-Request Triage

Generated: 2026-06-11

Status: research only. No outreach, spend, applications, live integrations, or
external commitments made.

## Opportunity

Insurance agencies, especially commercial-lines and independent agencies, handle
high-volume service requests: certificates of insurance, policy document
questions, renewal reminders, claims intake, and client follow-up. This is a
strong fit for JVT's bounded workflow model because the first sellable wedge can
stay in intake, routing, summarization, and review packets without issuing COIs
or making coverage decisions.

## Target Customer

- Independent insurance agencies
- Commercial-lines brokers
- Small regional agencies using email-heavy service workflows
- Agencies that receive frequent COI, renewal, endorsement, or claims-status
  requests

## Pain / Demand Signal

- The Big "I" reported in February 2026 that two-thirds of independent agencies
  plan to increase AI use in the next 12 months, while many are still early in
  adoption.
- Agency technology resources now include AI-specific training and service
  provider contract guidance, which suggests demand plus caution.
- COI requests are repeatedly described by vendors and trade sources as
  time-sensitive, document-heavy, and error-sensitive.

## Offer

`Insurance Service Request Triage`

JVT would set up a review-first workflow that:

- watches a designated inbox or intake form
- classifies requests such as COI, renewal, claim, billing, endorsement, or
  general question
- extracts structured fields for staff review
- drafts the next internal task and client response
- routes the packet to a licensed/staff reviewer
- logs status and unresolved missing information

JVT does **not** issue COIs, alter policies, advise on coverage, bind coverage,
or make licensed insurance decisions.

## Pricing Hypothesis

- Pilot: `$750-$1,500` setup for one inbox/request type
- Support: `$300-$750/mo` for workflow upkeep, prompt/rule maintenance, and
  monthly review
- Expansion: `$1,500-$3,500` setup if adding multiple request categories or
  agency-management-system handoffs

## Startup Capital

`$0-$250` for demo/proof work if using existing JVT infrastructure.

Paid provider cost only becomes relevant if a client approves live mailbox/API
access, phone intake, or third-party integration.

## Likely Time To First Revenue

2-6 weeks if packaged as a narrow paid pilot and aimed at small agencies with
public service/contact workflows.

## Delivery Complexity

Medium.

The non-negotiable design is human review. The first pilot should avoid direct
AMS writeback and instead produce review packets until trust and workflow fit
are proven.

## Major Risks

- Licensed insurance boundaries and E&O exposure
- Client privacy and document handling
- Agency-management-system integration complexity
- Staff distrust if automation appears to replace judgment
- Overpromising COI issuance speed
- Data-entry errors if extraction is not reviewed

## Next Validation Step

Build one synthetic proof asset:

`COI request email -> extracted fields -> missing-info checklist -> staff task -> draft client response`

Then create a no-contact prospect segment of 10 independent/commercial insurance
agencies with public service-request or COI language. Keep all outreach behind
normal recipient quality gates and explicit send approval.
