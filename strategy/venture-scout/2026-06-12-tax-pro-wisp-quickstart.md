# Venture Scout: Tax Pro WISP Quickstart

Generated: 2026-06-12

Status: research only. No outreach, spend, applications, live integrations, or
external commitments made.

## Opportunity

Tax preparers, CPAs, bookkeepers, and small accounting firms need written data
security programs that are tailored, accessible, reviewed, and kept current.
This is a practical JVT service wedge because it combines document generation,
workflow mapping, vendor inventory, staff acknowledgment, and recurring review
operations.

JVT should not sell this as legal/compliance certification. The safer offer is a
review-first implementation packet based on public IRS/FTC guidance, with final
approval owned by the firm and its professional advisors.

## Target Customer Or Sponsor

- Solo and small tax preparation firms
- CPAs and bookkeeping practices handling taxpayer/client financial data
- Firms with seasonal staff, remote access, cloud tax tools, document portals,
  or ad hoc vendor lists
- Existing JVT accounting/bookkeeping prospects that did not respond to generic
  AI workflow outreach

## Pain / Demand

- IRS guidance says tax professionals are required by law to create a Written
  Information Security Plan (WISP) to protect client data.
- IRS Security Summit guidance says tax and accounting professionals are treated
  as financial institutions under GLBA/Safeguards Rule obligations.
- FTC guidance says covered financial institutions must maintain a written
  information security program with administrative, technical, and physical
  safeguards appropriate to size, complexity, activity scope, and sensitivity of
  customer information.
- Small firms often know they need the plan but lack time to assemble asset
  inventories, vendor lists, staff access lists, incident-response workflows,
  review cadence, and acknowledgement records.

## Offer / Business Model

`Tax Pro WISP Quickstart`

One narrow fixed-scope implementation package:

- intake questionnaire for firm size, tools, staff, remote access, and vendors
- hardware/software/vendor inventory worksheet
- access and role matrix
- draft WISP packet aligned to IRS Publication 5708 structure
- incident-response workflow map
- staff acknowledgement/training tracker
- annual or quarterly review checklist
- optional monthly managed update support

Boundary:

- JVT does not provide legal advice.
- JVT does not certify compliance.
- JVT does not perform penetration testing or regulated security audits unless a
  qualified third-party partner is separately approved.
- Firm owner/advisor review is required before adoption.

## Startup Capital

`$0-$250`

Can start with existing JVT document-generation, workflow, and private intake
infrastructure. Paid security tooling, scanning, legal review, or partner
services should wait until a paying client approves scope.

## Likely Time To First Revenue

2-5 weeks if packaged as a limited-scope paid pilot for accounting/tax firms.
Fastest path is to reposition existing CPA/bookkeeping prospects around a
concrete required document/workflow rather than broad "AI automation."

## Pricing / Return Hypothesis

- Quickstart setup: `$750-$2,000`
- Annual refresh: `$500-$1,500`
- Monthly managed documentation/support: `$250-$750/mo`
- Partner expansion: refer technical controls, vulnerability scanning, or legal
  review to approved specialists if needed

This can support the March 2027 cash-flow target with a few paid setups plus
recurring refresh retainers.

## Delivery Complexity

Medium.

The hard parts are accurate scoping, security boundary discipline, and avoiding
overclaims. The first version should be a document/workflow implementation
packet, not a cybersecurity audit.

## Major Risks

- Legal/compliance overclaim risk if positioned as "guaranteed compliance"
- Cybersecurity liability if JVT implies technical control validation
- Sensitive client/taxpayer data handling
- Need for secure intake and storage
- Clients may expect hands-on IT remediation beyond documentation
- Regulations and guidance can change, so source dates matter
