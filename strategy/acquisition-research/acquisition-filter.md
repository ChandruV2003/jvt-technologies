# Small Business Acquisition Filter

Updated: 2026-06-10

Status: research only. Do not contact sellers, submit LOIs, share financial
details, pay deposits, or open marketplace accounts without approval.

## Target Thesis

Only consider acquisitions that can support the March 2027 cash-flow goal
without dragging JVT into a capital-heavy or operator-heavy mess.

The best fit would be:

- small B2B service revenue
- recurring or repeat customers
- simple operations
- clean books
- low fixed overhead
- customer pain that JVT automation can improve

## Automatic Rejection Rules

Reject listings that have:

- unverifiable revenue or profit
- seller add-backs that explain most of the profit
- concentration in one or two customers
- declining revenue without a clear cause
- heavy paid-ad dependency
- inventory, lease, vehicle, or labor complexity beyond budget
- required licenses the operator does not have
- vague "AI/passive income" claims
- no clean monthly financials
- seller refusing diligence

## Initial Criteria

- Asking price: only within explicitly approved capital range
- Payback target: under 24-36 months after conservative owner earnings
- Revenue: stable or growing for at least 12 months
- Owner time: clear weekly requirement
- Customer mix: no single customer over 25% of revenue
- Operations: documented process, tools, and handoff path
- Growth angle: automation or follow-up improvements can plausibly help

## Good-Fit Categories

- bookkeeping/admin support books
- small website maintenance retainers
- niche B2B service routes
- micro-SaaS with real customers and low support load
- content or lead-gen assets only if revenue source is transparent

## Bad-Fit Categories For Now

- restaurants
- retail inventory businesses
- crypto/NFT assets
- dropshipping stores
- high-churn agencies
- labor-heavy field services without management depth
- anything requiring urgent closing or seller financing pressure

## Next Validation Step

Create a no-contact watchlist only after this filter is approved or refined.
