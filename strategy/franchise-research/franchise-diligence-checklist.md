# Franchise Diligence Checklist

Updated: 2026-06-10

Status: internal research checklist. Do not request franchise information,
submit applications, pay fees, or contact franchisors without approval.

## First Pass Rejection Rules

Reject or pause any opportunity that:

- requires payment before reviewing an FDD or equivalent disclosure
- makes income claims without documented unit economics
- requires more capital than the operator explicitly approves
- requires full-time operation that conflicts with JVT priorities
- hides royalty, marketing, technology, training, or renewal fees
- requires territory commitments before diligence
- depends on labor or real estate assumptions we cannot support
- pushes urgency, scarcity, or "guaranteed income" claims

## Required Data Before Serious Review

- Total initial investment range
- Franchise fee
- Required liquid capital and net worth
- Royalty fee
- Marketing/ad fee
- Technology/platform fees
- Training cost and duration
- Required owner involvement
- Territory rights and restrictions
- Renewal/transfer rules
- Franchisee termination rights
- Unit count, closures, transfers, and litigation history
- Average revenue/profit claims only if disclosed properly
- Working capital need for first 6-12 months

## Fit Scoring

Score each candidate 1-5:

- Capital fit
- Speed to first revenue
- Recurring revenue potential
- Local demand
- Operational simplicity
- Labor burden
- Brand strength
- Territory quality
- Exit/transfer flexibility
- Conflict with JVT

## Chick-fil-A Specific Notes

Chick-fil-A is not a passive franchise investment. The official opportunity is
a selective operator model with low upfront fee but full-time hands-on
commitment and restrictions around conflicting active business ventures.

Treat it as monitored optionality only unless the operator explicitly wants a
full-time restaurant operating path.
