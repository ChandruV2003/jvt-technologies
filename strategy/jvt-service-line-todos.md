# JVT Service Line To-Dos

Updated: 2026-06-24

Each service line stays separate so research, demos, outreach, pricing, and delivery risk do not blur together. Public site positioning was added and deployed as the first completed step for all five tracks.

## 1. Meeting-to-action packets

- Target customer: consultants, agencies, CPA firms, advisory teams, and service businesses with recurring calls.
- Pain: useful call details turn into scattered notes, missed follow-ups, and manual task entry.
- Offer: audio or meeting recording to transcript, summary, follow-up draft, task checklist, and client-ready packet.
- Pricing hypothesis: $300-$1,000/mo depending on monthly volume and review needs.
- Delivery complexity: low-medium.
- Risks: transcript accuracy, consent expectations, meeting-platform access, and privacy handling.
- Completed: added public website card under Other Offerings.

- Completed: published the public meeting-to-action demo and proof PDF.
- Completed: generated proof-backed follow-up packets for verified accounting/advisory prospects.

## 2. AI receptionist and intake

- Target customer: solo and small professional offices that miss calls or repeat the same intake questions.
- Pain: missed calls and weak intake notes slow follow-up and leak opportunities.
- Offer: inbound AI intake that discloses itself, captures caller details, qualifies the request, and routes a review packet.
- Pricing hypothesis: $500-$2,000 setup plus usage and $300-$1,000/mo support.
- Delivery complexity: medium-high.
- Risks: phone compliance, hallucinated answers, failed escalation, live-call quality, and provider setup.

## 3. Managed AI workflow cleanup

- Target customer: law, accounting, property management, staffing, and operations teams already experimenting with AI.
- Pain: staff use AI, but outputs still require heavy cleanup, checking, and formatting.
- Offer: convert ad hoc AI usage into repeatable workflows with inputs, review checkpoints, source checks, and handoff notes.
- Pricing hypothesis: $750-$2,500 setup plus $500-$1,500/mo support.
- Delivery complexity: medium.
- Risks: scope creep, unclear ownership, and clients expecting unlimited custom automation.
- Completed: added public website card under Other Offerings.

## 4. Inbox and document triage

- Target customer: law firms, CPA firms, insurance offices, property teams, and admin-heavy service businesses.
- Pain: shared inboxes mix client requests, attachments, notifications, and follow-ups without a clean task queue.
- Offer: classify messages, extract attachment details, create tasks, and prepare draft replies for review.
- Pricing hypothesis: $1,500-$5,000 setup plus $500-$2,500/mo support.
- Delivery complexity: medium.
- Risks: mailbox permissions, privacy, email authentication, and edge cases in attachment handling.

## 5. Private or local AI assistant

- Target customer: privacy-sensitive professional teams with client documents and cautious technology policies.
- Pain: teams want AI search and drafting help but do not want sensitive files scattered across generic SaaS tools.
- Offer: controlled retrieval assistant and workflow console on private cloud, Mac mini, mini-PC, or approved server path.
- Pricing hypothesis: $3,000-$10,000 setup plus support retainer.
- Delivery complexity: high.
- Risks: support burden, hardware variability, security expectations, and longer sales cycles.
