# JVT Finance Setup Checklist

Use this when JVT is ready to move from "the commercial path is designed" to "money can actually move cleanly."

As of April 14, 2026, the recommended first stack is:

- Mercury for business banking
- Stripe for invoices and payment collection
- Wave Starter or QuickBooks Online Essentials for bookkeeping

## 1. Confirm Business Prerequisites

Before opening anything:

- confirm the exact legal entity name
- confirm the EIN letter or EIN confirmation
- gather formation documents
- gather owner and operator government IDs
- confirm the real business address and phone number you want on applications

If these are not ready, stop here. That is the real blocker.

## 2. Open The Bank Account

Recommended first choice:

- Mercury

Why:

- clean early-stage banking setup
- free core account
- good fit for a U.S. service business
- easy connection point for Stripe payouts

Goal at the end of this step:

- live business checking account
- routing number
- account number
- ability to receive ACH and payouts

## 3. Open Stripe

Use the real legal entity information, not placeholder data.

Goal at the end of this step:

- JVT Stripe account verified
- business profile completed
- payout account connected to Mercury
- invoice branding set to JVT Technologies

## 4. Choose The Books System

Start with one of these:

- Wave Starter if the goal is lowest cost
- QuickBooks Online Essentials if you want cleaner reconciliation and accountant-friendly books earlier

Do not run both in parallel as the primary ledger.

## 5. Define Payment Policy

Use the JVT defaults unless there is a real reason not to:

- fit call: free
- discovery: due on receipt
- pilots and fixed-scope work: 50% upfront, 50% at milestone or delivery
- retainers: billed upfront monthly
- payment terms: due on receipt or Net 7
- ACH preferred, card accepted

## 6. Run An Internal Payment Test

Before invoicing a real client:

- create one self-test Stripe invoice
- send it to yourself
- pay it yourself
- confirm payout lands in Mercury
- confirm the transaction is recorded in the books system

Do not skip this. It is the safest way to prove the system actually works.

## 7. Confirm The Commercial Templates

Before the first real client invoice, make sure these are ready:

- proposal or scoped pilot summary
- engagement letter or services agreement
- invoice memo language
- payment terms language
- support or milestone language if applicable

## 8. Start With Small, Clean Invoices

For the first few clients:

- keep line items simple
- keep terms simple
- do not overcomplicate taxes, discounts, surcharges, or billing logic
- prefer clarity over cleverness

## Recommended Order For JVT

1. legal entity and EIN confirmed
2. Mercury opened
3. Stripe opened and connected
4. books system chosen
5. internal self-test invoice completed
6. first client deposit invoice sent
