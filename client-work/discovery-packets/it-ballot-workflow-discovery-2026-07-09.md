# IT Ballot And Board-Meeting Workflow Discovery Packet

Generated: 2026-07-09

Status: internal discovery packet. Do not send, process live ballot data, draft election notices, publish results, or make service commitments without explicit approval.

## Target Customer

IT consulting / AV operations company supporting housing-complex board meetings, owner communications, ballot administration support, and election-adjacent document workflows.

## Pain

- Board meeting prep creates repeated checklists, agendas, packets, notices, and follow-up tasks.
- Ballot support creates deadline tracking, eligibility documentation, document versioning, and audit-sensitive handoffs.
- Information sits across inboxes, spreadsheets, PDFs, meeting notes, and client-specific folders.
- Mistakes are high-trust problems even when the task is "just admin."

## Offer

A review-first agentic operations layer for meeting prep, ballot workflow checklists, document drafting, status tracking, missing-info detection, and audit logs.

The system does not determine voter eligibility, certify results, send official notices, count ballots, make legal decisions, or replace required human/client review.

## Discovery Questions

- What exact services does the company provide before, during, and after board meetings?
- Which parts are AV/IT logistics versus ballot/election support?
- What documents are repeatedly generated?
- What spreadsheets, PDFs, inboxes, portals, or shared drives are used?
- Who approves notices, packets, instructions, and results?
- What data is sensitive: owner names, addresses, unit numbers, eligibility, proxies, signatures, email addresses?
- What must be retained for audit history?
- What is the riskiest workflow that should be excluded from the pilot?
- What is the safest low-risk workflow that still saves time?

## First Low-Risk Pilot

Do not touch live ballot data. Start with meeting-prep and packet-drafting support:

- Intake checklist for a new board-meeting engagement.
- Missing-information tracker.
- Draft agenda and prep packet from synthetic source notes.
- Task list for AV setup, document prep, client approvals, and post-meeting follow-up.
- Audit log showing who reviewed each generated artifact.

Optional second pilot only after trust: ballot timeline checklist using synthetic deadlines and placeholder voter/owner data.

## Pricing Hypothesis

- Discovery/workflow map: $500-$1,500.
- Narrow pilot build: $2,500-$7,500.
- Managed AI ops retainer: $500-$2,000/month depending on meeting volume, document complexity, and QA.

## Delivery Complexity

Medium-high. Admin automation is straightforward, but election-adjacent workflows require explicit human approval, auditability, permission controls, and careful language.

## Risks

- Legal/compliance ambiguity around housing-complex election processes.
- PII and owner/voter eligibility data handling.
- Scope creep into a full election-management platform.
- Client expects the system to make official decisions.
- Audit logs must be credible and easy to export.
